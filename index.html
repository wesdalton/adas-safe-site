Frequently Asked Questions

What is ADAS Calibration and when is it necessary?

+

ADAS Calibration refers to the precise process of aligning and adjusting the Advanced Driver Assistance System (ADAS) in modern vehicles to ensure optimal performance and safety. It becomes necessary after events like windshield replacements, bumper repairs, or other services that might disrupt the sensors or cameras integral to the ADAS. Calibration ensures that these systems function correctly, enhancing driving and parking safety.

How often should I get ADAS calibration?

+

While there isn't a one-size-fits-all answer, you should consider ADAS calibration after routine services like windshield or bumper replacements, after collisions, or any body repairs involving sensor areas. Moreover, activities like vehicle painting or replacing parts near the ADAS sensors and cameras may also necessitate calibration. At ADAS Safe, we recommend consulting with our experts to determine the best calibration schedule for your specific vehicle and driving conditions.

What's included in your Electronic File Documentation?

+

Our Electronic File Documentation, accessible through our Cloud-Based Portal, includes photo documentation of calibration phases, and the current calibration procedure. This documentation emphasizes our commitment to transparency, accuracy, and accountability for every calibration performed.

Do you provide diagnostic scanning and module programming?

+

Yes, at ADAS Safe, we provide comprehensive diagnostic scanning and module programming. We can reprogram any replacement ADAS module, ensuring its seamless integration with your vehicle's systems. We also provide wire/harness repair services.
